I agree with you that the way he broke up the work into sections and part worked well. I strangely liked the lack of chronological order because it allowed for Cressy to add *Vast* amounts of information into each chapter without being overwhelming. For example, I loved chapter five because it felt like “Changes in the Land” by William Cronon to me without losing all the information and stylization throughout the rest of the book. The only major issue I had was the Latin? quotes that were in the book and either not translated in a footnote, or translated in the notes toward the end of the book. That fits a much more academic and formal audience, but also just a pet peeve of mine.
