]]> By: Logan Skorupa https://2022hist635.jessicaotis.com/2022/03/21/the-murder-of-king-james-i-book-review-2/#comment-63 Thu, 24 Mar 2022 00:17:13 +0000 https://2022hist635.jessicaotis.com/?p=585#comment-63 Hi Vincent, to be completely honest I got the exact opposite conclusion from this book.
It is my understanding that James I died from his illness with the possible assistance of the medical treatments he received. The accusations of regicide committed by the Duke of Buckingham and the new kings ‘complicity’ were initially spread by the Duke of Buckingham’s adversaries were coopted by the domestic and foreign enemies of Charles I. The prologue to part II (page 65-67) gave some examples of how rumors of the health and even death of the monarch were commonly spread regardless of the actual status of the monarch and reflected the anxieties present in political systems dependent on the health of one singular individual. Chapters 4 and 5, it seems to me discredit the author of “The Frontrunner” by detailing his willingness to engage in underhanded activities and his subsequent expulsion from England.
The main point that I got from this book was that people in England (specifically those connected to Parliament) were extremely dissatisfied with Charles I and his war with Spain thanks to the strain it placed on England’s economy and Charles unwillingness to work with Parliament and seized on the rumor of king James ‘murder’ to justify the removal of Buckingham from power and later undermine the legitimacy of king Charles once the English Civil war was well under way. Combine this with the undercurrent of religious tension that saw Catholics go into hiding and anonymously criticize the state church for being too Protestant while Puritans criticized the King for not being Protestant enough, I do not find it surprising at all that people who were constantly paranoid about ‘crypto-catholic/popeist plots’ were willing to buy the idea that a king they disliked murdered the previous king to seize power earlier than he ‘should’ have.
